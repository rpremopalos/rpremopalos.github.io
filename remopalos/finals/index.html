About Sibulan

A Gateway to Paradise

Sibulan is a first-class municipality in the province of Negros Oriental, Philippines. Known as the gateway to Dumaguete City, Sibulan is home to the Sibulan Airport and offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and warm hospitality.

The town is blessed with scenic coastal areas, lush mountains, and vibrant local traditions. Visitors can experience authentic Filipino culture while enjoying modern amenities and conveniences.

Location: Negros Oriental, Central Visayas

Population: Approximately 60,000

Founded: 1850

Top Attractions

Discover the Natural Wonders of Sibulan

Twin Lakes of Balinsasayao

Experience the pristine beauty of these crater lakes surrounded by lush forest, perfect for hiking and kayaking adventures.

Coastal Beaches

Relax on beautiful white sand beaches and enjoy crystal-clear waters ideal for swimming and snorkeling.

Historic Churches

Visit centuries-old churches that showcase Spanish colonial architecture and local religious heritage.

Mountain Viewpoints

Hike to scenic viewpoints offering breathtaking panoramas of the surrounding countryside and coastline.

Sibulan Airport

A unique spot where you can watch planes take off and land while enjoying local snacks and scenic views.

Food & Accommodations

Taste and Stay in Sibulan

Local Cuisine

Must-Try Dishes
  • Budbud Kabog: Traditional rice cake wrapped in banana leaves
  • Fresh Seafood: Grilled fish, squid, and prawns caught daily
  • Lechon Manok: Perfectly roasted chicken with local spices
  • Lechon Belly: Frozen cookie sandwiches, a Dumaguete specialty available here

Where to Stay

Accommodation Options
  • Beach Resorts: Beachfront properties with stunning ocean views
  • Budget Hotels: Comfortable and affordable lodging near the airport
  • Homestays: Experience local hospitality in family-run guesthouses
  • Eco-Lodges: Sustainable stays near mountain attractions

Events & Festivals

Celebrate with the Community

Sibulan Town Fiesta

Date: September 29

The annual town fiesta celebrating the patron saint with street dancing, cultural performances, food festivals, and community gatherings.

Pasigarbo sa Sibulan

Date: Various dates throughout the year

Cultural celebration showcasing traditional dances, music, arts, and crafts from different barangays of Sibulan.

Agricultural Fair

Date: November

Showcase of local agricultural products, farming innovations, and agri-tourism opportunities in Sibulan.

Christmas Celebrations

Date: December

Experience the Filipino Christmas spirit with Simbang Gabi, parol making, caroling, and holiday markets.
